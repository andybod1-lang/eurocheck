Frequently Asked Questions

How do you determine if a company is European?

We research ownership structures using public filings, company registries, and verified sources. A company is classified as "EU" if its ultimate parent company is headquartered in a European Union member state. When ownership is complex (joint ventures, partial acquisitions), we mark it as "mixed" rather than guess.

What data does EuroCheck collect?

None. Absolutely zero. EuroCheck runs entirely in your browser using a local database bundled with the extension. We have no servers, no analytics, no telemetry. We couldn't see your browsing data even if we wanted to.

Which browsers support EuroCheck?

EuroCheck works on Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave, Opera, and other Chromium-based browsers. Safari support is planned for a future release.

How accurate is the company database?

Our database covers 321+ companies and is continuously updated. Each entry includes a confidence level. We prioritize accuracy over quantity ‚Äî when we're uncertain, we say so. Community contributions via GitHub help us stay current.

How can I suggest a company to add?

Open an issue or pull request on our GitHub repository. Include the company name, website, headquarters location, and any sources you have about ownership. Community contributions are always welcome!

Is EuroCheck really free?

Yes, 100% free. No premium tiers, no "pro" version, no hidden costs. EuroCheck is a community project built to empower consumers, not monetize them. If you want to support development, you can buy us a coffee.
